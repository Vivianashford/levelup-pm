Sam Ovens' Complete Methodology

Consulting.com - The Operating System for Building a Consulting Business

1. The Avatar - Get Specific Until It Hurts

Don't say "business owners." Say "Mike, 58, owns a plumbing company doing $12M, has 85 employees, hasn't taken a real vacation in 6 years, his wife is pushing him to slow down, he got a call from a PE firm last month and didn't know what to say."

"Can you describe your ideal client so specifically that if they walked into a coffee shop, you'd recognize them?"

Your avatar isn't a demographic. It's a person with a specific pain at a specific moment in their life.

Key questions to define your avatar:

  • What keeps them up at 2 AM?
  • What have they already tried that didn't work?
  • What would they pay almost anything to solve?
  • Where do they hang out (online and offline)?
  • What triggered them to start looking for help NOW?
2. The Offer - Solve the Gap, Not the Symptom

The offer isn't "exit advisory." The offer is the BRIDGE between where they are and where they want to be.

Current State
Working 60 hours. Can't take a week off. Worried about valuation. Business dies without them.
→
Dream State
90 days off. Business grows without them. Sell for 6-8x not 3x. Legacy protected.
The GAP between current and dream - THAT is what you're selling. Price is never the objection when the gap is clear. A $200K fee against a $3M valuation increase? No-brainer.

Your offer must answer:

  • What specific result do they get?
  • In what timeframe?
  • What's included?
  • What makes it different from everything else they've seen?
  • Why should they trust YOU to deliver?
3. The Iteration Cycle - Scientific Method of Consulting

No awesome offer was created on the first guess. It's ALL iterations.

Hypothesize
"I think X wants Y"
→
Test
Get on 10 calls
→
Feedback
What did THEY say?
→
Iterate
Adjust + repeat

Diagnostic questions (don't sell - DIAGNOSE):

  • "What made you take this call today?"
  • "What have you tried before?"
  • "If we could wave a magic wand, what would your business look like in 18 months?"
  • "What's stopping you from getting there yourself?"
  • "What would solving this be worth to you?"
4. The Message - Mirror, Don't Manufacture

Your marketing should sound like your client talking to their spouse at 11 PM.

❌ Wrong
"We provide comprehensive exit advisory services leveraging proven methodologies."
✓ Right
"You built this thing from nothing. You deserve to walk away on your terms, knowing it's in good hands and you got what it's worth."
The best copy is stolen - from your prospect's mouth. Every sales call, write down their exact phrases. Those become your ads, your emails, your VSL.
5. Minimum Viable Offer - Pre-Sell Before You Build

Don't build the full 18-month program and THEN sell it. Pre-sell the outcome, deliver the first phase, use that feedback to build the rest.

For The Arena: Phase 1 (Align) is the minimum viable offer. Charge for it. Deliver it. Use Jet Lending's experience to refine Phases 2-4 before selling the full system to client #2.

Every single data point from Jet Lending is worth more than 100 hours of planning. Document everything. What worked. What they pushed back on. What made Eddie's eyes light up. That IS your product refinement.

Provide as little of you and your time as possible. That's not lazy - that's scalable. Package the transformation, not the hours.

6. The 5 Layers of Business
Layer 1 - Awareness: Can you see your patterns, beliefs, and feedback loops clearly?
Layer 2 - Cognition: How do you think? Focus, prioritization, first principles.
Layer 3 - Principles: Rules of operation. One niche, one offer, pre-sell, market feedback over theory.
Layer 4 - Disciplines: Full-stack: product, marketing, sales, delivery, operations.
Layer 5 - Processes: Tools and workflows. Only after the foundation is solid.
7. First Principles - The Bedrock
Market is the only lab. Your mind, friends, family are fiction when it comes to testing ideas. Only the market tells truth.
Foundation leverage. One small improvement in foundations = massive downstream impact.
Simplify to the equation. Everything boils down to math. Find the formula, optimize variables.
Data beats opinions. You can't scale what you can't measure. Track everything.
Week one before Facebook ads. Foundations before tactics. Always.
8. Sam's Priority Hierarchy
1. CLOSE DEALS - Anyone waiting on a proposal, follow-up, or decision? That's #1.
2. SALES CALLS - Anyone booked or ready to book? Get on the phone.
3. OUTREACH - No deals in pipeline? 100 conversations is the only fix.
4. OFFER REFINEMENT - Getting on calls but not closing? Fix the offer.
5. CONTENT / SYSTEMS - Only after 1-4 are handled. Never first.

Phase 1 - Mindset & Foundations: Clear vision, first principles, daily structure. ✓ Done

Phase 3 - Offer Creation: Transferable Exit System - $150-200K + success fee. ✓ Done

Phase 5 - Sales & Closing: Diagnostic calls, NDA, company info, close. Two-call system. Active
