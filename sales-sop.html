📍 THE 4 PHASES OF ENGAGEMENT
Phase 1: Alignment (Weeks 1-2)

Kickoff call - align on vision, timeline, priorities.

Pre-visit prep (auto-included in welcome email):
- Key people to meet (ops lead, sales, admin, foremen)
- Systems to get access to before arrival
- Financial records needed
- Daily operations schedule overview

Phase 2: On-Site Immersion (Weeks 3-5) - GO TO CLIENT LOCATION

You go to their location for 2-3 weeks. This is the differentiator.

- Meet every key team member one-on-one
- Shadow daily operations to see how the business actually runs
- Document every process that lives in the owner's head
- Map org chart - who does what, who's replaceable, who's not
- Identify owner-dependent bottlenecks
- Evaluate management team's readiness to run without owner
- Interview top clients (with approval) to assess relationship risk
- Audit financial systems, reporting, cash flow

Why this matters: A buyer will do their own version during due diligence. We do it first so there are zero surprises. This is what separates us from every other "exit advisor" who just sends templates.

Phase 3: System Build (Weeks 6-12)

- Build SOPs for every undocumented process
- Create the operations manual a buyer needs
- Implement management dashboards and KPIs
- Transfer owner-dependent relationships to team
- Train the number two on everything the owner does alone
- Financial clean-up: separate personal from business, identify add-backs

Phase 4: Market Ready (Months 4-18)

- Prove business runs without owner (3-6 month track record)
- Optimize EBITDA for maximum valuation
- Prepare data room for buyers
- Position for strategic premium
- When ready: introduce to qualified buyers or broker

👤 PRE-VISIT CHECKLIST (included in auto-welcome email)
📋 Block 2-3 weeks on calendar with client
📋 Get list of key people to interview (ops lead, sales, admin, foremen)
📋 Request access to: financial systems, CRM, project management tools
📋 Request: employee handbook, existing SOPs, org chart, vendor contracts
📋 Book travel and accommodations
📋 Prepare interview templates for each role
🎉 This is the goal

📅 MONTH 1 - CONSCIOUSNESS RAISING (Days 1-30)
6 emails + 4 SMS + 1 handwritten notecard (Handwrytten)

Goal: Make them conscious of the gap between where they are and where they need to be.
Jay Abraham: Preeminence. Position Jean as the most trusted advisor they've never hired.

Handwritten notecard via Handwrytten - personal, stands out in the mail. Sent end of Month 1.

Copy style: Education-first. No pitch. Build the case that their business has a time bomb if owner dependency isn't fixed before exit.

📅 MONTH 2 - DRAMATIC RELIEF (Days 31-60)
4 emails + 3 SMS + 1 oversized postcard (PostGrid) + 9-word email (Day 35)

Goal: Create emotional urgency. Show them the pain of inaction vs. the relief of moving forward.
Jay Abraham: Risk Reversal. Make the decision to engage feel safe and obvious.

Day 35 - 9-word email (plain text, no branding, no images):
Subject: quick question
Body: "Are you still looking to exit your business, [First Name]?"

Oversized postcard via PostGrid - physical mail cuts through digital noise. Includes a specific case result.

📅 MONTH 3 - SELF-REEVALUATION (Days 61-90)
3 emails + 3 SMS + 1 personalized letter (PostGrid)

Goal: Help them see themselves differently. Not just as a business owner - as a future seller.
Jay Abraham: Education-Based Marketing. Teach them what buyers actually look for.

Personalized letter via PostGrid - full letter, addressed to them by name. Feels like something important arrived.

Copy style: "Here's what a buyer's due diligence team will look at when they walk into your building. Are you ready for that conversation?"

📅 MONTH 4 - URGENCY AND BREAKUP (Days 91-120)
4 emails + 3 SMS + 1 premium handwritten card (Handwrytten) + Day 112 nine-word email + Day 120 breakup

Goal: Create urgency. This is the last chance to re-engage before the file closes.
Jay Abraham: Scarcity + Pattern interrupt.

Day 112 - 9-word email (plain text, no branding):
Subject: still thinking about this?
Body: "Wanted to check in one last time, [First Name]."

Day 116 - Conditional SMS: Only fires if lead score > 50. "Hey [First Name], just one thing left to tell you before I close your file - want to hear it?"

Premium handwritten card via Handwrytten - highest quality card stock, personal note from Jean.

Day 120 - BREAKUP EMAIL:
Subject: Closing your file, [First Name]
Body: Plain text. No pitch. Graceful exit. "I've been following up for 4 months because I genuinely believe we could add millions to your exit. But I don't want to keep bothering you. I'm closing your file today. If anything changes, you know where to find me."
